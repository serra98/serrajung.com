TechTogether Boston 2019

Post by Serra Jung, April 28, 2019.
CS

I had an amazing opportunity to attend TechTogether Boston event as a volunteer and helper. TechTogether Boston is previously known as SheHacks Boston which has been the largest student-run hackathon open to all female and non-binary individuals. This event was cherishable as it really emphasized unity and inclusivity within the tech industry related to women and non-binary individuals. I also learned lots of insights into the way of approaching problems.

    The mission of this event was to Empower women and non-binary individuals in technology to achieve
    Provide them with opportunities to explore the tech industry in an inspiring, encouraging, and energizing environment
    Create a community of inclusivity within the tech industry
    Sponsors: BU Spark, Innovate@BU, wayfair, toast, Fidelity investment, redhat, Microsoft, Dell, Atlassian, SAP, JP Morgan Chase & Co, IBM Research, Bloomberg, MathWorks, John Hancock, Google, Facebook, CapitalOne, BookBub, and Shutterstock

Boston Code Camp BCC30

Post by Serra Jung,April 28,2019.
CS

Code Camps have spread around the world and strengthened many local developer communities. This Boston Code Camp was held in Microsoft in Burlington, MA for the IT developers to share knowledge with fellow developers in an open format and an informal setting. This was a very good way for me to communicate with a different age group of people not only college students. Few of the most interesting workshops I've taken here was "Azure Web Applications" and "How to build WebAssembly apps with Microsoft's stack". It was an eye-opening moment for me to explore more topics than reading algorithms back in college.

    Sponsors: Microsoft, Pulsar security, Mill5, vistaprint, slalom, RGood software

Code for Good in Jersey City

Post by Serra Jung,April 28,2019.
CS Github

I had an amazing opportunity to participate in Hackathon called Code for Good in Jersey City. It was held by JP Morgan Chase with few rounds of chosen applicants and I was honored to be one of the coders to hack in New Jersey. This is where I've first developed my interest in front-end development and learn more about React Native, HTML, and CSS. We were able to collaborate with other coders to develop innovative technology solutions for non-profit organizations based on New York City. Our team chose to make a responsive website over the mobile application for better accessibility for a low-income family.

    Sponsors: JP Morgan Chase Co
